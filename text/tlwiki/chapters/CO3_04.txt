As far as I know, this facility doesn't have any red rooms.

"cold" "dark" "locked up" "all alone"

『赤い部屋』『入る』%K%P
"red room" "enter"

There, Hotori hung her head, all worn out.

The pen fell to the floor.

Don't strain yourself.

I laid Hotori down on the bed.

おとなしく従ってくれた。%K%P
She docilely complied.

穂鳥が書いた言葉……。%K%P
The words Hotori wrote....

私には、なんとなく思い当たる節があった。%K%P
They somehow rang a bell.

赤い部屋というのは、穂鳥の心の中の風景、あるいは心の中で感じる感覚ではないだろうか。%K%P
The red room, is it scenery from inside Hotori's heart, or could it be something she felt she saw within her mind?

ＤＩＤでは、ある人格から別の人格に変化する時、表層から消える人格は、暗い部屋の中にポツンと置き去りにされたような感覚を抱くそうなのだ。%K%P
It is said when people with DID switch personalities, the personality that is neglected feels like it was left alone in a dark room.

その感覚が、穂鳥の言う『寒くて』『暗くて』『閉じこめられてて』『ひとりぼっち』という状態ではないか。%K%P
That feeling, isn't it the sense of "cold," "dark," "locked up," "alone" like Hotori said?

そして人格交代が起こるとき、表に出る人格は、暗闇から明るい部屋へと移動する。%K%P
And when the switch occurs, the surfacing personality moves from the darkness to a bright room.

実際に明るい部屋があるのではなくて、『そうとしか表現しようのない感覚』が心の中のどこかにあるらしい。%K%P
In practice, it's not a bright room, but a "usually inexpressible sensation" now somewhere in her mind.

その部屋はつまり、外界と繋がる象徴的風景だと思われる。%K%P
That room, in other words, seems to be part of the symbolic scenery related to the physical world.

患者によっては『明るい部屋』ではなくて、『スポットライト』など別の比喩表現をされることもある。%K%P
According to the patients, it's not a "bright room," but is represented with another figurative expression: "spotlight."

それが、穂鳥にとっては『赤い部屋』なのだとしたら。%K%P
If we consider that to Hotori, that's the "red room"...

典型的なＤＩＤの症例だ。%K%P
It's a typical DID case.

でも穂鳥は、自分がＤＩＤであることを理解できていない。%K%P
But Hotori can't understand that she has DID.

だから混乱しているのだ。%K%P
So she's confused.

私なんかよりも、よっぽど苦しい思いをしているに違いない。%K%P
I'm sure she has very painful thoughts, even more than I do.

なんとか、彼女の苦しみを和らげてあげたいけれど……。%K%P
I want to somehow ease her pain, but....

私の力では、こうして手を握ってあげることくらいしかできない。%K%P
With my strength, I can do no more than keep holding her hand like this.

It's all right.

Just one more time, I murmured those same words.

気休めでしかないけど、言わずにはいられなかった。%K%P
They were nothing more than mere consolation, but I couldn't help saying them.

こころ「大丈夫だからね」%K%P
